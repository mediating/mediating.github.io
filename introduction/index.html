mediating media arts
introduction / people / speculative camp satellites


While the history of Media Art has seen the growth of an international network of travelling exhibitions, curators, artists, and artworks, there has hardly been any exchange among media art educators internationally. This knowledge gap has been the starting point for the research project Mediating Media Arts, funded by Goethe Institut and the UNESCO City of Media Arts Karlsruhe, with the goal of initiating an intercultural discourse about art education practices in relation to media art, both inside and outside media art museums.

With different institutional histories, educational systems, and cultural funding policies, understandings of media art mediation vary from timezone to timezone. We research practices of art education in relation to media art and technology. We are curious to understand how art education is institutionalized and in what frameworks it is situated in (with regard to technology, education and cultural policy in different timezones). What are the pressing issues for media art mediation practices with regard to different local contexts? Are post-internet art education, creative coding, issues of privacy and identity, and critique of technology relevant to all approaches? How can media art mediation balance technology, energy, community, and ecology? And to what extent has the covid pandemic intiated digital mediation formats and hybrid experiments?
