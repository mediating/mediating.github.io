Hanna Hirakawa
#people

Who are you, where do you live and work?

I am Hanna Hirakawa. Curator at the Idemitsu Museum of Arts, specializing in Japanese Edo Period Art. I also work on independent projects that deal with archives and contemporary art in Asia concerned with its (colonial) history and sociopolitical issues. Translator and interpreter in the field of contemporary art. Shorter version: Hanna Hirakawa. Curator at the Idemitsu Museum of Arts. Works on independent projects dealing with archives and contemporary art in Asia. Translator in the field of contemporary art.

How would you define mediating media arts / media art mediation?

How media art (particularly what is called new media art, but may include art of any medium, broadly speaking) is intercepted and used to communicate, create connections, engage in collaborative activities. The MMA is the also the name of the collective project working to understand and explore the issues as well as new possibilities of mediating media arts (the above) through interdisciplinary perspectives.

What specific issues and concerns do you have in the field of education and technology?

How can we communicate cultural contexts or culturally specific issues by the means of technology? How can technology be adopted in both useful/un-useful ways in mediating learning in the museum environment?
